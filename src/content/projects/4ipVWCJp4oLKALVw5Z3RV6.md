## How it started


Intuit's products (Quickbooks, Turbotax, and Mint) used surprisingly little data visualization. I led a 2-year research initiative to shape the future of financial data visualization, coached by founder Scott Cook.


The Innovation & Advanced Technology group had a mission: inspire and influence the company with game-changing design and technology. Since I was working in a central group, and wasn’t part of a specific product, part of the challenge was finding the right collaborators and enthusiasm in each of the product teams (Quickbooks, Turbotax, Mint, etc) to help create a stairway to the future with a long-term vision and product ideas we could implement today.


Much of my focus ended up on Artificial Intelligence as a method of data delivery, since the IAT group was filled with brilliant machine learning engineers and product owners.


## Learning & Evolution


- How to get comfortable presenting (to 400+ people)

- Lots of new things about data visualization from some of the greats: Edward Tufte & Alberto Cairo (among others)

- How to work in a large company with cross functional business units from a central organization to influence the product roadmap

- Merging financial data with artificial intelligence to help power individual prosperity
