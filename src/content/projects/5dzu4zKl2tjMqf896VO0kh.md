## How it started

## How it started



As Polycam scaled beyond initial product success, we needed formal product management processes, team structure, and growth strategy to transition from startup to sustainable growth company.


The company lacked career development frameworks, systematic experimentation processes, and clear product strategy. The challenge was building organizational maturity while maintaining the innovation pace and scrappy culture that made us successful.

## Learnings

## Learning & Evolution


This role taught me that scaling product leadership requires balancing strategic vision with operational excellence. Culture and people development are as important as product decisions for long-term success.


Since this was the second time I was able to build out a team, I used a lot of what I learned at Matterport to create a better product and design culture, while maintaining a business focus.


This experience confirmed my readiness for product leadership roles at larger organizations with more complex challenges. The combination of design thinking, strategic planning, and organizational development creates a unique perspective on product leadership.
